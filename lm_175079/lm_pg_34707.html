Essay structure

What is the structure of an essay? What rules do I have to follow?

Watch the following short video to learn the basics of structuring your essay.
The most typical essay is a 5-paragraph one, with the first paragraph being the introduction to the topic, the second, third and fourth the body paragraphs where the ideas are developed, and the fifth paragraph giving the concluding remarks.

A good essay is often described as a burger - the bun, salad, cheese and patty all being separate parts but coming together for a tasty whole.

Note that this is certainly an oversimplified structre and if you are feeling comfortable enough with writing, there is no need to follow these guidelines to a T and to write a 5 paragraph essay as such. You can be a lot less rigid in your approach, while still adhering to the overall rules stated below.

If you would like to find out more about what the problems with the 5-paragraph essay are, there is a very infomrative video about it HERE.

The same author suggests a more organic and less rigid structure for essays HERE.
However, there are certain general rules that you should keep in mind when organising your essay, no matter if it is a 5-paragraph one or a more organic one:
  • in the introduction give general introductory remarks on the topic and state what you are going to talk about
  • try to raise the readers' interest in the introduction
  • in the body paragraphs present your ideas on the topic
  • only develop one idea in each paragraph - do not mix different ideas in one paragraph
  • in the conclusion summarize what you have said and do not introduce any new ideas
  • only include relevant information that is directly connected to the topic
  • do not repeat the same ideas in different paragraphs
  • all paragraphs should be of more or less equal length
  • the ideas presented in different paragraphs should be of equal weight and importance
  • keep the tone neutral and style formal
  • connect all ideas into one complete whole by using linking devices and moving smoothly from one point to the next both between sentences and between paragraphs.
