Before writing

How do I get started? What steps do I take?

The process of writing an essay is not much different from the process of writing anything else. The main difference is, that as a piece of academic writing an essay requires concentrating more heavily on certain norms. In the following chapters we will take a look at the most important of those norms and requirements.
Often people find it harder to write an essay than anything else. The reason seems to be the type of thinking that is needed and the topics that are provided. As we usually do not think in such abstract terms and on such theoretical topics, it is hard to come up with the content for the essay. Hopefully the following information will help you get started. The basic steps you should follow when writing an essay are:
  • analysing the topic
  • brainstorming ideas
  • organising and clustering (mindmapping) ideas
  • removing and adding information
  • providing additional and supporting details
  • linking ideas
  • writing up paragraphs
  • writing an introduction
  • writing a conclusion
  • revising
  • editing

Next, we will look at more specific steps you need to take to come up with an essay plan or outline.

Let's take an example essay topic of "What are the most important aspects when choosing a job?"
Note that this is a personal opinion essay, rather than a research-based academic one and thus the process is slightly simpler. 

After you have analysed the topic and determined the main theme and question, you should brainstorm for any ideas connected to the topic. This can be done on paper or in any mind-mapping tool online.

After you have organised and clustered your ideas it is time to look them over critically - add more details and remove repetitive or too detailed/general ones and come up with a more specific plan, where you choose the 3 topics (for 3 body paragraphs) that you wish to concentrate on.
In this mindmap the ideas are grouped and organised into paragraphs.



As you can see you now have three separate themes for the body of the essay. The introduction and conclusion will be the last you write, based on the body paragraphs. But at the moment you have three topics with enough ideas and supporting detail to get started. Based on this type of clustered and organised information you can start creating the body of the essay.
